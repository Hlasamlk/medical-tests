Types of medical tests

  • Medical analyzes:

    There are a large number of types of medical analyzes, which are used in various medical fields, such as assessing the health status of the individual, diagnosing many diseases and health problems, detecting genetic diseases, and identifying the efficiency of the functioning of body organs such as the kidneys and liver, and will be in this article Talking about some types of medical tests in some detail.

  • types of medical analyzes Routine laboratory tests Complete blood count:

    The complete blood count-CBC analysis is the test that measures the cells that make up the blood, such as: red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets, and is used to diagnose anemia. Blood, and in detecting health problems that cause headaches, fever and fatigue, and to follow up on the effect of treatments and medications such as: chemotherapy, and it should be noted that this type of examination does not require any prior preparation.

  • Urine examination:

    which is a set of physical, chemical, and microscopic tests that measure some substances in urine, such as: glucose, protein, bilirubin, red blood cells, white blood cells, crystals, bacteria, etc., and helps diagnose and monitor many diseases, such as: problems Kidney and urinary tract infection, or if some symptoms appear, such as: abdominal pain, back and frequent urination, in addition to using it in pregnancy examination, and in cases of pre-surgical follow-up, and to obtain an accurate result, the woman is advised to clean the area well before giving the sample, and the man should To wipe the tip of the penis before starting to urinate, and the sample should not be taken at the beginning of the urination process, but rather in the middle of it, and sent to the laboratory within a short period. In the event that the sample took more than an hour to arrive, the urine sample must be cooled or inquire from the specialized technician.

  • Stool examination:

    Stool analysis is used to diagnose some allergic conditions of the body, such as: detecting milk protein allergy in infants, and in cases of infection caused by bacteria, parasites, and viruses that infect the digestive system, and when problems occur in the digestive system, such as: malabsorption of nutrients, and to determine The type of bacteria or parasite infecting the intestine. Most of the time, the pathogenic bacteria or parasite can be identified from examining one sample, but sometimes three samples are taken from different stool times to confirm the diagnosis.

  • Blood chemistry tests

    Blood sugar test:

    It is called checking blood glucose levels, glucose is a type of sugar, in some cases blood glucose levels may rise dramatically and this condition may be a symptom of diabetes, or it may decrease from normal levels and cause major health problems such as : Brain damage if it is not treated, it may be necessary to fast for eight hours before the test, and in another type of blood sugar tests such as: checking blood sugar levels when pregnant, a sugary drink is drunk one hour before the blood draw.

  • Cholesterol and lipid test:

    A lipid test is commonly used to assess the risk of cardiovascular disease, and it is necessary to fast for 9-12 hours before drawing a blood sample. This type of test detects total cholesterol and LDL cholesterol. -density lipoprotein, good cholesterol and triglycerides, and experts recommend monitoring cholesterol levels every five years, on a routine basis. Cholesterol tests frequently, as advised by your doctor.

  • Kidney function tests:

    Kidney function analysis is used to identify and diagnose kidney problems, or in cases of diseases that may harm the kidneys, such as: diabetes and high blood pressure, and among these tests:

    • Serum creatinine test:
      which examines the accumulation of creatinine in the blood, as the kidneys completely filter creatinine from the blood.
    • Blood urea nitrogen test:
      checks for the presence of waste products in the blood, and works to measure the amount of nitrogen in the blood.
    • Renal filtration rate (GFR):
      This test measures the ability of the kidneys to filter waste.

  • Liver function tests:

    Liver function tests are blood tests that determine whether the liver is working properly or not. These tests include:

    • Serum bilirubin test: It is an examination that measures the levels of bilirubin in the blood, which is formed by the liver. High levels of it indicate obstruction to the flow of bile, and therefore the possibility of a problem with the liver.
    • Serum albumin test: This test measures the level of protein in the blood, or as it is called albumin. If the test result is less than normal, it indicates that the liver may have a problem.
    • Clotting time test: This test measures the time it takes for blood to clot, and since the blood clotting process needs vitamin K and protein that are synthesized in the liver, an increase in clotting time may indicate the presence of liver diseases, in addition to enzyme tests that give indications of how well the blood is working. liver.
    • Alanine aminotransferase: This enzyme is elevated in viral hepatitis and some other liver diseases, and may be elevated before the onset of disease symptoms.
    • Aspartate aminotransferase: It is an enzyme found mostly in the liver. If the liver is damaged or damaged, its levels in the bloodstream rise.

  • Immunoassays:

    Immunological tests measure antibodies made by the immune system to fight infection causes such as: bacteria and viruses, which give the doctor important information about the functioning of the immune system and autoimmune diseases. These tests reveal:

    • IgA: which is found in the mucous membranes and in saliva and tears.
    • IgG: which is found in all body fluids, and works to protect the body from bacterial and viral infections.
    • IgM: which is mainly found in lymph fluid and blood.
    • IgE: mainly associated with allergic reactions. Immunoglobulin
    • IgD: It is the least common antibody in the blood.

  • Hormonal examinations:

    hormones have many basic functions in the human body. If any hormonal imbalance occurs in the body, whether the level of one of the hormones becomes more or less than the normal limit, and even if it is slight, this may have serious effects on the health and functions of the body, and some symptoms begin to appear, which indicate With a difference in hormone levels, the doctor orders hormonal blood tests, such as: levels of thyroid hormones, estrogen, testosterone, cortisol, and others.
